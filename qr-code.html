Frequently Asked Questions

What data types can I encode in QR codes?

DevUtilities supports all standard QR code data types including plain text, URLs, email addresses, phone numbers, SMS messages, WiFi credentials, contact information (vCard), and custom data formats.

What are error correction levels and which should I choose?

Error correction allows QR codes to remain readable even when damaged. Low (7%) for clean environments, Medium (15%) for general use, Quartile (25%) for outdoor use, and High (30%) for harsh conditions or small print sizes.

What sizes are available for QR codes?

DevUtilities generates QR codes in multiple sizes suitable for different applications: small (for business cards), medium (for flyers), large (for posters), and extra large (for banners and outdoor signage).

Can I scan QR codes with DevUtilities?

Yes! DevUtilities includes a built-in QR code scanner that uses your Mac's camera to decode QR codes instantly. Perfect for testing your generated codes or reading existing ones.

What image formats can I export?

QR codes can be exported as high-quality PNG images in various resolutions. PNG format ensures crisp, scalable images perfect for both digital use and print materials.

Are the generated QR codes compatible with all scanners?

Yes, DevUtilities generates standard QR codes following ISO/IEC 18004 specifications, ensuring compatibility with all QR code scanners and mobile camera apps.
