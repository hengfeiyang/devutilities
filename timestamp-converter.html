Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Unix timestamp?

A Unix timestamp is the number of seconds (or milliseconds) that have elapsed since January 1, 1970, 00:00:00 UTC. It's a standard way to represent time in computer systems and is timezone-independent.

Does DevUtilities handle millisecond timestamps?

Yes! DevUtilities automatically detects whether your timestamp is in seconds or milliseconds and converts accordingly. It supports both formats seamlessly.

How does timezone conversion work?

DevUtilities includes comprehensive timezone support with automatic daylight saving time adjustments. You can convert any timestamp to your local time or any other timezone worldwide.

Can I see my conversion history?

Absolutely! DevUtilities automatically tracks your conversion history, making it easy to reference previous conversions. This is especially useful when working with multiple timestamps.

What date formats are supported?

DevUtilities supports multiple date formats including ISO 8601, RFC 2822, custom patterns, and locale-specific formats. You can choose the format that best fits your workflow.

Is the current timestamp always visible?

Yes, DevUtilities displays the current Unix timestamp and formatted date in real-time. This is perfect for generating current timestamps for testing or development purposes.
